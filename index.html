    About me

    I am an undergraduate sociology student at Pontificia Universidad Católica del Perú (PUCP), currently in my last year of studies. My main interests are directed towards computational social science, with an special emphasis in statistical network analysis. In this area, I have experience working as research assistant for professors Phd(c) Cesar Castillo-García and Msc. Luz Sánchez Pérez, in a project in which I helped with the application of network analysis to study the relationships among peruvian economic-policy makers from 1930 to 1990.

    In addition to this, I have received the Scholarship for Academic Excellence from PUCP (BEA) as well as the academic merit award in the semester 2021-1. More recently, I received an scholarship from the Artificial Intelligence and Computational Methods Laboratory in Social Sciences (QLAB), which allowed me to take extension courses in Python, Machine Learning and Econometrics.

    During my academic life, I have also engaged in extracurricular activities, such as extension courses in statistics and network analysis, volunteerings, and a presentation at the XXVI Colloquium of sociology students
